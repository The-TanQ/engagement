1. Eye Strain and Comfort

Many users report that dark mode reduces eye strain, especially in low-light environments. Staring at a bright white screen in a dark room can be harsh, whereas dark mode offers a more comfortable reading experience. However, in bright sunlight, light mode often proves superior, providing better visibility and readability.

2. Battery Life

On OLED and AMOLED screens, dark mode can extend battery life significantly because black pixels require no backlight. This makes dark mode not just an aesthetic choice but also a practical one for users who need longer device usage between charges. Light mode, while consuming more power, can still be more suitable in office or outdoor environments.

3. Accessibility and Usability

Accessibility is an important consideration. Some users with vision impairments or dyslexia may find dark mode difficult to read, while others find it improves clarity. The best approach is to offer both options, allowing users to choose what works best for their eyes and environment. Inclusivity improves user satisfaction across the board.

4. Psychological Effects

Colors and contrast affect mood. Dark themes can feel immersive and cinematic, while light themes often appear more professional and business-oriented. Developers working late at night may favor dark mode, while professionals reviewing reports might lean towards light mode for clarity.
